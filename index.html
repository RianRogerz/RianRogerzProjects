Projects

Electronic CCAR


This was my first project I started on. By having an idea to convert Jaguar Land Rover's way of dealing with prototype vehicle issues on paper into an electronic method. I initially learnt Javascript through Google scripts on Google sheets and created an improved version of the paper system. This system had timestamps, metric reports of data, automatic emails to engineers of their issues and image/video upload capability.
From our platform migrating from Google Apps to Office 365 I had to learn VBA which enabled me to create an improved replica which became self sustainable. This was done by creating code to input data into tables and then the scripts would recreate a new file with the new builds information and automatically create files and connect them together with Microsoft Access and Excel.
This work won me my first Individual innovator of the year out of many applicants throughout JLR. I ended up implementing this system into several plants around JLR, all prototype plants in England - Whitley, Browns Lane and Castle Bromich pilot builds. I even travelled to Slovakia to implement the system at their new billion pound facilities pilot build plant.


Electronic Build Books


The Electronic Build Book was my 2nd big system that I developed. Whilst I was implementing my eCCAR in Slovakia me and the team I was with decided its time for our paper builds books to be electronic. On our return we worked together to figure out exactly what was needed.
I then created the electronic build book which was usable on iPads. The build book files were automatically generated and inputted the vehicle specification and build plan timings. I created a PIN system for technicians to sign their zone off for build start and build finished. This created a dot chart for seniors to track the vehicles progress electronically. The build book also stored important information like shortages, critical checks and in process containment checks for the technician to sign that they've completed them as they build the cars.
This won me my 2nd individual innovator of the year award 2 years after the first one I won.


Shortage Scanning System


When doing a very important prototype build there was loads of shortages coming in late due to late parts. As there were so many shortages there had to be a lot of deliveries. Unfortunately there was no way of tracking this so a lot of parts went missing when being delivered to the build zone which meant the part was lost and then we required another part.
Working along side the leader of stores logistics we came up with a solution that required us to use coding to read a part number by scanning the part with a scanner and then pulling the data out of our stores system to retrieve the parts information, and then a way of logging it to the zone it was delivered to with a date and time.
Using VBA in excel I created a form which would pop up when the file loads allowing the shortage part number to be scanned, it then opened up the stores system and pulled out the data. Another form opens after this data is inputted for the zone to be logged. Other barcodes were made for each zone which were scanned upon zone delivery. This then inputted the zone name into the file along with a datestamp after being confirmed that the zone leader had retrieved the part. It then reopened the original form for the next shortage to be scanned and so forth.
This meant there were no lost parts for future builds and gave us a way of logging the amount of shortages per build enabling us to create graphs and reports out of the data.

At this convention I was demonstrating the functionality of these systems and presenting our goals to achieve through coding and making these systems work together.
